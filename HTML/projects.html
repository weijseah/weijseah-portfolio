Sim Racing Cockpit

After participating in a couple of High Performance Driver Education (HPDE) events in my Honda Prelude at Calabogie Motorsports Park, I quickly realized how much there was to learn about driving a car quickly around a track. As much as I wanted to continue improving, more seat time was not financially sustainable given my student budget.

I figured that it would be nice to pick up a wheel to play a racing simulator on my computer as I work towards finishing my degree. This lead to my purchase of a Thrustmaster T300RS wheel, along with a copy of rFactor 2 to play on PC. My setup at the time consisted of a folding chair, with my wheel clamped to my computer desk. At first, my pedal set slipped along the hardwood floor whenever I would press the brake pedal. To mitigate this issue I placed an anti-slip mat underneath the pedal set, but now the front of the pedals would lift off of the floor whenever I pressed hard on the brakes since the pedal set would pivot off of the back side. In addition, the strength of the force feedback from the T300RS wheel caused my whole computer desk to shake, leading to many objects falling off of my desk while playing.

Clearly, there was much to be improved about my setup. I wanted to design and build a steel frame to mount all of my equipment. My roommate at the time was available to provide some advice for the design, and to help me with the machining and manufacturing work that would be needed to complete this project. To start, we determined an ergonomic seating position while I sat in a Recaro seat that my brother generously lent to me. From here we got an idea of the relative dimensions needed between the seat, wheel and pedals.
